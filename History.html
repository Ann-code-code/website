Excelsior! A little History.
Marvel Comics was established in 1939 by Martin Goodman, a magazine publisher at that time.

This inspired Martin Goodman to create Timely Comics and so the birth of the Marvel Universe began.
The first comic book was Marvel Comics no.1 and saw the Human Torch (not Johnny Storm of the later Fantasic Four fame but an android) and the Sub-Mariner first appear.
During the “Golden Age” of the 1940's, many superhero characterss were created such as; Captain America.
World War II saw Captain America and other comic book heroes, fight against Nazis and the 'Axis of evil'.
Unfortunately, by the end of the 1940's superhero comics were no longer popular, so Timely ended the superhero run in 1950.
Martin Goodman changed Timely Comics to Atlas Magazines in 1951 after starting his own distribution company along with his publishing company.
DC Comics commercial success in 1956 spurred Martin Goodman to try again with his own superhero comics again.
In the early 1960s Atlas changed its name to Marvel Comics.
For several decades Marvel and DC were the top companies in the industry.
Stan Lee and Jack Kirby were taken into the Marvel fold in 1961.
This was the start of the comic books and superheroes we know and love today. Superheroes such as; The X-Men, Fantasic Four, Spiderman and of course The Hulk! And many more...
